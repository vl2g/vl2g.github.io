Abstract

IndicPhotoOCR is a scene text recognition toolkit designed for detecting, identifying, and recognizing text across Indian languages, including Assamese, Bengali, Gujarati, Hindi, Kannada, Malayalam, Marathi, Odia, Punjabi, Tamil, Telugu, and English, with support for Urdu and Meitei in the pipeline. It is built to handle the unique scripts and complex structures of Indian languages, offering robust detection and recognition capabilities. The package can be installed with just few lines of code, and a straightforward wrapper function makes it easy to use.

 

Detection

For the text detection task, we utilize TextBPN++ (Zhang et al., 2023), a state-of-the-art boundary-based text detector. TextBPN++ initially generates a coarse boundary proposal around each text instance, which serves as a preliminary estimate of the text region. This initial boundary is then progressively refined through an iterative optimization process that adjusts the boundary points to better conform to the actual text contours. To evaluate the quality of the detected boundaries, we employ TedEval, a metric specifically designed to assess the precision of text boundary predictions by comparing them to ground-truth annotations.

Cropped Word Script Identification

After obtaining the bounding boxes, regions were cropped to get word level image.Further script identification task was studied under two distinct settings: (A) distinguishing between three major script categoriesâ€”English Script, Hindi Script, and Regional Language Scripts (such as Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, etc.), and (B) training a single unified model capable of recognizing all 12 individual scripts. To explore the effectiveness of different architectures, experiments were done where AlexNet and CRNN were trained from scratch for the task, CLIP and ViT were fine-tuned on our script-labeled dataset to better adapt their pre-trained visual representations to the specific characteristics of script data. Fine-tuning allowed powerful transformer-based models to leverage their prior knowledge while learning the nuanced differences across scripts, especially in the multilingualsetting of the study.

Cropped Word Recognition

After identifying the script of the text region, the next step involves recognizing the actual word content within the cropped text images. For this, we employed PARSeq, a robust transformer-based model for scene text recognition. Since different languages may have distinct character sets and writing patterns, a script-specific PARSeq model is selected based on the predicted script. This targeted approach ensures that the most appropriate language model is applied for recognition, thereby improving the accuracy and reliability of the word recognition process.

 

Dataset

  • BSTD: Bharat Scene Text Dataset

    Bharat Scene Text Dataset is large and real Indian language scene-text dataset with coverage across 13 Indian languages and English. It consists of 6,582 scene-text images, with polygon bounding box annotations of 1,20,560 words and ground truth text annotations of 1,00,495 cropped words.
