Not Ready Yet: A Reality Check on AI Models for Automatic Handwritten Answer Sheet Grading

Gyan Prabhat1, G. Sai. Vassista2, Anand Mishra1
1Indian Institute of Technology Jodhpur, 2Keshav Memorial Engineering College Hyderabad
Paper arXiv Code Data

Abstract

In recent years, AI models, particularly Vision-Language Models (VLMs), have demonstrated strong capabilities in document understanding, including handwritten text recognition. These advances naturally raise the question of whether such models are now ready for real-world educational tasks such as automatic fine-grained numerical grading of handwritten answer sheets. In this work, we study this problem and investigate how current VLMs compare against human evaluators. Despite its practical importance, this problem remains under-explored. To facilitate research in this direction, we introduce HA-101, an introductory-level benchmark comprising 745 real classroom handwritten answer sheets with human-assigned scores. To improve grading performance, we propose a hierarchical prompting strategy that strengthens the reasoning and evaluation capabilities of VLMs through prompt engineering. We evaluate 5 state-of-the-art open-source and closed-source models on this benchmark. Our results show that current models still suffer from both over-grading and under-grading errors, indicating that fully autonomous deployment is not yet reliable. To address this limitation to some extent, we further propose a human-in-the-loop framework that combines AI efficiency with human oversight for more dependable grading.
